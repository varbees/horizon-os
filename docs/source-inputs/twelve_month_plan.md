# 12 Months: From Cluster to Clarity

A plan to forge the instrument *and* dissolve the one who claims it.

---

## The Frame

You said only one being exists, experiencing itself. That stays at the center. Everything below is in service of that — not decoration around it.

Two questions run in parallel for the next 12 months:

- **Foreground:** What gets built? (Offgrid life, capital, body, skill.)
- **Background:** Who is building it? (The witness behind the doer.)

They are not separate tracks. The body is forged so it can be offered. The capital is built so the small self stops gripping it. The land is bought so attention has somewhere quiet to land. Hanuman carried mountains to carry Rama. Same logic.

The three figures you named are the three doors:

- **Hanuman** — dissolution through service. Strength becomes infinite when no "I" claims it.
- **Krishna (Gita Ch. 2)** — act fully, drop attachment to being the doer. *Karmanyevadhikaraste ma phaleshu kadachana.*
- **Baki Hanma** — forge the instrument so ferociously that the whining self has nothing left to say.

Pick one as your touchstone for each phase. Or rotate.

---

## The Four Anchors

Everything in the next 12 months collapses into four columns. If a thing doesn't serve one of these, it's noise.

### 1. Body — the instrument
Non-negotiable daily floor. Yoga, breath, strength, food. The body is what carries the project. If the body breaks, the plan breaks.

### 2. Attention — the witness
Daily sit. Self-inquiry (*Who am I?* — Ramana's method, not a slogan). Phone-off hour. This is where the background question gets worked on. Without this, you're just rearranging the cluster, not seeing it.

### 3. Capital — the runway
Money is freedom-fuel for the offgrid move. Income, expenses, savings, and the specific runway-to-land number. Track it weekly. No fuzz.

### 4. Spec — the destination
A real, costed, location-specific offgrid plan. Land, water, power, food, shelter, internet (if any), legal. By month 6 this is a spreadsheet, not a feeling.

---

## The 12-Month Arc

Three phases, four months each. The character of each phase shifts.

### Phase I (Months 1–4): Forge
**Touchstone: Baki.** Build the instrument. No romance about offgrid yet — just the body, the discipline, the income engine. This phase is brutal and unglamorous.

- **Body:** Yoga or movement daily, minimum 20 min, no exceptions. Two strength sessions/week. Clean food. By month 4, fitness is visibly different.
- **Attention:** 30 min daily silent sit. Start with breath, then move to *Who is sitting?* No apps. Same time, same spot.
- **Capital:** Track every rupee/dollar in and out. Compute monthly burn. Compute: *months of runway I currently have* and *months of runway I need to go offgrid*. The gap is your real number.
- **Spec:** Read. Watch offgrid builds. Identify 2–3 candidate regions. No purchases yet.

**Phase I exit gate:** Daily practice is unbroken for 30+ days. Capital number is exact. Two regions shortlisted.

### Phase II (Months 5–8): Test
**Touchstone: Krishna.** Act, drop attachment to outcome. Test reality against fantasy. Most offgrid plans die here, and that's the point — better to find out now.

- **Body:** Add endurance — walking, hiking, carrying weight. The land won't care about your gym aesthetic. It will care if you can work 6 hours outdoors.
- **Attention:** Extend sits. Add a weekly half-day of silence. Begin journaling the inquiry — not feelings, just what the question is showing you.
- **Capital:** Income engine should be producing real money. If it isn't by month 6, you have a problem worth naming — not a plan worth continuing. Be honest.
- **Spec:** Visit shortlisted land in person. Sleep on it. Talk to people who already live offgrid in that region. Costed spreadsheet by end of month 8: land + setup + 12 months living buffer.

**Phase II exit gate:** Income covers current life + saves toward offgrid number. One region chosen. Spec spreadsheet exists with real numbers, not estimates.

### Phase III (Months 9–12): Transition
**Touchstone: Hanuman.** Service. The move itself. Quiet, strong, no theatrics.

- **Body:** Maintenance. Don't peak now — you'll need the energy for the move.
- **Attention:** This is where the inquiry meets the test. Moving offgrid will surface every unexamined fear. Sit through them, don't run.
- **Capital:** Close the gap. Liquidate what doesn't move with you. Carry runway.
- **Spec:** Acquire. Build/move. Set up water, power, basic food systems. Month 12: first night on your own land.

**Phase III exit gate:** You're there.

## Daily Cadence (the floor, not the ceiling)

Same every day. Sundays included.

- **Morning (before phone):** 30 min sit. Then 20–40 min body.
- **Workday:** Deep work on the income engine. Phone off for at least one 90-min block.
- **Evening:** 15 min review. Three lines in a notebook: *What did I forge today? What did I see today? What did I avoid?*

The morning sit before the phone is the single most important habit. If you only keep one thing, keep that.

---

## Weekly Review (Sunday, 30 min)

Five questions. Same five, every week.

1. Did I keep the daily floor? (Yes / mostly / no — be honest.)
2. What moved on the four anchors? (Body, Attention, Capital, Spec.)
3. What's the runway number this week? Did the gap shrink or grow?
4. What was I gripping that I don't need to grip?
5. What's the one thing for next week that, if done, makes the week count?

---

## Monthly Recalibration (last Sunday of month)

Three deeper questions.

1. **Who was I this month?** Not what I did — who showed up. Was it the witness or the cluster?
2. **What's the most honest version of the plan right now?** Plans must be allowed to update. Reality > the document.
3. **If I had to cut one thing to make the rest stronger, what would it be?** Subtraction is the discipline most people skip.

---

## Failure Modes to Watch

- **Spiritualizing avoidance.** Long sits, no income progress. The inquiry isn't a hiding place. Krishna told Arjuna to *fight*, not to meditate his way out of the battlefield.
- **Forging without dissolving.** Pure Baki mode produces a stronger ego, not a freer one. The sit is the counterweight.
- **Romanticizing offgrid.** Offgrid is mostly water systems, weather, and boredom. If the fantasy can't survive a costed spreadsheet, it won't survive the actual land.
- **Solo isolation.** "One being experiences itself" is a metaphysical claim, not a social instruction. Keep two or three people in your life who can tell you the truth.

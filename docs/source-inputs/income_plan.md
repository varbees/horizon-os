# Income Pathway: 12 Months to Offgrid

For: technical builder, full-time available, no steady floor.
Against: the 2026 market as it actually is.

---

## The Numbers You're Actually Solving For

Most people skip this. Don't. The plan changes based on the target.

Realistic offgrid budget (rough, location-dependent — dial in during Spec work):

| Item | Range |
|---|---|
| Land | $5k – $80k |
| Water (well, storage, filtration) | $5k – $15k |
| Power (solar + battery + backup) | $10k – $30k |
| Shelter (build or tiny home or refurbish) | $20k – $100k |
| Food systems (garden, livestock setup) | $3k – $10k |
| 12-month living buffer offgrid | $20k – $40k |
| **Total cash needed** | **$60k – $250k** |

Plus you need **ongoing MRR** to live offgrid. Offgrid is cheaper than urban life, not free. Realistic floor: **$2–3k/month** indefinitely.

**Your 12-month target:**
- **Cash:** $80–150k accumulated (middle estimate; tighten when Spec is real)
- **MRR:** $3k/month recurring that survives the move

If your number is much lower (e.g. you own land already, or are going rural-but-light in a low-cost country), recalibrate down. If much higher, the plan stretches to 18 months — don't lie to yourself about timeline.

## The Brutal Frame

With no income and full-time available, two enemies will kill this plan:

1. **Anxiety.** No floor = bad decisions. You'll take the wrong client, price too low, panic-pivot in month 3. Floor first.
2. **Drift.** 40+ hours, no boss, no structure. Without weekly targets, six months disappear into "building" with nothing shipped.

The plan handles both by **stacking**: floor → engine → product → exit. Each phase funds the next.

The single most common failure mode for technical builders: going straight to "I'll build a product." Product income is 6–18 months away minimum. You'll be out of cash before it earns its first dollar. **You will service first, then product. Not in parallel at the start.**

## Phase 1 (Weeks 1–4): Lock the Floor

**Goal:** $5–10k of contract income hitting your account within 30 days.

**Mechanism:** AI-augmented contract dev work. You can deliver 2–3x a normal contractor's output by using Claude/Cursor/etc. as multipliers. Charge accordingly.

**Pricing:** $80–150/hr depending on stack and seniority. Do not go below $80. If you've been undercharging on platforms before, this is the reset.

**Week 1 actions:**
- Rewrite your LinkedIn and personal site around *one specific outcome you deliver* (e.g. "I ship AI-integrated MVPs in 4 weeks for B2B SaaS founders") — not your resume.
- List 50 specific target companies / individuals who likely need this. Use real names.
- Send 20 personalized cold messages. Not templates. Each one references something specific about them.
- Tell every person in your existing network you're available, with the specific offer.

**Week 2–4 actions:**
- 20 more personalized outreaches per week.
- Take the first 1–2 contracts that come in even if not perfect — the floor matters more than the perfect fit. Filter from week 5 onward.
- Track: contacts made → replies → calls → signed. You're calibrating a funnel.

**Exit gate for Phase 1:** Signed contract(s) generating $5k+/month. If you're at zero in week 4, the offer is wrong — change it, don't grind harder.

---

## Phase 2 (Months 2–5): Build the Service Engine

**Goal:** 2–3 retainer clients at $5–10k/month each. By month 5: $15–25k/month in retainers.

**Mechanism:** Productized service in a specific niche. Stop selling hours. Sell *outcomes* in a fixed scope.

Example productized offers (pick one, get sharper from here):

- "AI Integration Sprint" — 4 weeks, $15k, ship a working AI feature into a B2B SaaS company's product.
- "RevOps Automation Retainer" — $8k/month, automate a sales/ops team's repetitive workflows.
- "Vertical SaaS MVP" — $25–40k, custom internal tool for a non-tech industry (HVAC, dental, construction, ag).
- "Data Pipeline for AI-native Startups" — $10k/month retainer, build and maintain the data plumbing.

**The niche selection logic:**
- Buyers have money (B2B > B2C, established > seed-stage).
- Buyers have pain you can name in one sentence.
- You can reach them (community, LinkedIn, a list exists).
- The work is repeatable across clients (so you can productize and later product-ize).

**Distribution that works in 2026:**
- **Cold outbound** still works for B2B if it's specific and short. LinkedIn DM > email > Twitter DM. Not blasts — targeted, hand-written.
- **Build in public** on Twitter/X or LinkedIn — post your work, what you're learning, what you ship. Slow compound, but starts paying by month 4–5.
- **Communities** — Indie Hackers, specific Slack/Discord groups for your niche. Helpful presence, not pitching.
- **Warm intros from clients** — once you have one happy client, ask. This is the highest-conversion channel and most people don't ask.

**Exit gate for Phase 2:** $15k+/month in retainers, 2+ months of expenses banked, you've turned down at least one bad-fit client.

## Phase 3 (Months 4–8): Find the Wedge, Productize

**Goal:** Identify the repeatable pain across service clients and build a small SaaS or tool around it.

**Mechanism:** You're now seeing 2–3 clients deal with similar problems. One of them is automatable. Build it.

This phase overlaps with Phase 2 — you keep delivering service, but spend ~10 hours/week on the product wedge.

**The wedge criteria:**
- A pain you've solved manually 3+ times for clients.
- Solvable as a focused tool (single core feature, not a platform).
- Your service clients would buy it. (Best validation: pre-sell it to them.)
- Buildable in 2–4 weeks of focused work.

**Pricing the product:** $30–300/month depending on whether it's individual-buyer or B2B. B2B can pay more. Skip "free tier" at first — you're validating willingness to pay, not building user base.

**Distribution for the product is the niche you already know** — same community, same outbound, same playbook. You don't need a new audience.

**Exit gate for Phase 3:** Product live, 10+ paying customers, $1–3k MRR by month 8.

---

## Phase 4 (Months 8–12): Product Grows, Service Tapers, Move Happens

**Goal:** Product MRR hits $3k+/month. Cash position is at target. Land bought. Move executed.

This is where the philosophical frame from the main plan matters most. You'll be tempted to:
- Take "one more big service contract" that delays the move
- Over-build the product
- Spiral about offgrid logistics

**The discipline this phase:** Service tapers deliberately (don't take new retainers after month 9). Product gets minimum viable polish (resist feature creep). Land + setup gets primary attention.

By month 12: service is closed out or trimmed to 1 client, product MRR is sustaining, you're sleeping on your land.

## Specific Niches Worth Considering in 2026

Pick one. Going broad kills the funnel.

| Niche | Why it works |
|---|---|
| AI integration for B2B SaaS | Every SaaS co. is trying to ship AI features, most can't execute well. High pay, urgent. |
| Vertical SaaS in unsexy industries (HVAC, dental, ag, construction) | Owners have money, hate software, will pay for custom tools. Underserved. |
| Automation/RevOps for sales-led startups | Recurring pain, recurring spend. Retainer-friendly. |
| Developer tooling | Technical buyers, willing to pay, you understand them. |
| Data infra for AI-native startups | Funded buyers, urgent need, high ticket. |
| Agency-of-one for solo founders | Specifically helping non-technical founders ship — high empathy fit, fast trust. |

If you have prior domain knowledge (industry, language, geography), that's the right niche by default. Don't pick the trendiest one — pick the one where you know the buyer.

## Distribution Playbook (the part nobody actually does)

This is what separates the plan that works from the plan that doesn't.

**First 10 conversations (target by end of week 2):**
- 10 calls with potential buyers in your niche. Not pitches — questions.
- Ask: What are you trying to ship? What's blocking you? What have you tried? What would you pay to make this go away?
- Three of these become first clients. The other seven sharpen your offer.

**Weekly outbound minimum:** 25 personalized messages. Track in a sheet. This is non-negotiable through month 4.

**Content cadence:** 3 posts/week on your chosen platform. Document what you're shipping for clients (anonymized). Slow compound — by month 6, inbound starts.

**The single best move:** Once you land a client, over-deliver on the first engagement, then ask for two specific intros. 80% of long-term pipeline comes from this.

## Traps Specific to Technical Builders

- **Building before selling.** You will want to do this. Don't. Sell, then build to fulfill what you sold.
- **Charging hourly forever.** Productize after 2 contracts. Hourly caps your income at hours.
- **Going broad to "keep options open."** Options are the enemy. Pick one niche by end of week 2.
- **Optimizing the stack.** You don't need a new framework. You need 25 outbound messages.
- **Solo silence.** You will spiral in your own head. Pick one accountability partner, weekly 30-min call. Not optional.
- **Romanticizing the product.** Product is the back end of the plan, not the front. Service funds you. Product frees you. Wrong order = bankruptcy.
